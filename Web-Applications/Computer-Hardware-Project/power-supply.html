POWER SUPPLY UNIT (PSU)

Model: ATX Standard

Definition & Function

This is the heart of the PC. It pumps electricity to all the components. It takes the high voltage from your wall plug and converts it into lower voltages that the computer can use without burning up.

Assembly & Disassembly

Step 1: ORIENTATION

The PSU goes usually at the bottom or top of the case. Look at the fan on the PSU. It needs to breathe. If your case has a vent on the bottom, point the fan down. If not, point it up into the case so it doesn't suffocate.

Step 2: SECURING

Push the PSU into its corner. Then, from the outside back of the PC case, use 4 screws to lock it in place. It's heavy, so make sure all screws are tight.

Step 3: CABLES

Route the cables to the back of the case to keep things tidy. Connect the main ones first: the 24-pin to motherboard and 4/8-pin to CPU. Then connect SATA power to drives and PCIe power to the graphics card.

Maintenance & Warning

Clean the dust filter below the PSU often. WARNING: Be careful if you open the metal box of the PSU itself. Even if unplugged, the capacitors inside hold electricity and can kill you.
