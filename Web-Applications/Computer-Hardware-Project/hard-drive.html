HARD DRIVE (HDD)

Model: SATA 500GB

Definition & Function

This is the long-term memory of the computer. Unlike RAM, when you turn off the PC, the data stays here. It's like a library full of books. It holds your Windows, your photos, and your games.

Assembly & Disassembly

Step 1: THE BAY

Look for the metal cage usually at the front-bottom of the case. This is the drive bay. Slide the HDD into an empty slot. Make sure the connectors are facing towards the inside of the case (towards the motherboard), not the front cover.

Step 2: SECURING IT

Once inside, use the small screws to secure it to the side of the metal cage. HDDs vibrate when they work, so if you don't screw it in, it will make noise and might break.

Step 3: CABLES

You need two cables here. First, the SATA Data cable (usually red and flat), which goes to the motherboard. Second, the SATA Power cable (flat and wider), which comes from the power supply. They have an "L" shape, so they only fit one way.

Maintenance & Tips

Mechanical hard drives are fragile. Never move or shake the computer while it is turned on, or the needle inside could scratch the disk and you will lose everything.
