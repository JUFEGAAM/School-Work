MOTHERBOARD

Model: Generic ATX Board

Definition & Function

The motherboard is the skeleton of the computer. It connects everything together. Without it, the CPU couldn't talk to the RAM or the hard drive. It distributes the power and provides the roads (circuits) for data to travel.

Assembly & Disassembly

Step 1: PREPARING THE CASE

Before putting the board in, make sure the case is empty. You must install the "standoffs" (little copper legs). This is super important: if you screw the motherboard directly to the metal wall of the case without these legs, you will cause a short circuit and fry it.

Step 2: PLACEMENT & SCREWS

Gently slide the motherboard in until the screw holes match the copper legs. Screw them in.

Step 3: CONNECTIONS

Now comes the messy part. You have to connect the huge 24-pin cable for power, the smaller CPU power cable (usually top left), and all the tiny cables for the front buttons (Power SW, Reset SW).

The CPU Socket

This is where the processor lives. It has hundreds of pins (LGA) or holes (PGA). It has a metal lever to lock the CPU in place.

RAM Slots (DIMM)

Long slots usually located to the right of the CPU. They have clips at the ends. This is where you stick the memory modules. Most boards have 2 or 4 slots.

PCIe Slots

These are for expansion cards. The longest one (x16) is almost always for the Graphics Card. Shorter ones (x1) are for Wi-Fi or Sound cards.

Rear I/O Panel

The back part of the motherboard that sticks out of the case. It contains the USB ports, Ethernet (LAN) jack, and audio jacks.

The Chipset

Usually covered by a heatsink (a metal block) in the bottom right corner. It controls the traffic between the CPU and the slower peripherals like USB and SATA.

Maintenance & Tips

Check the capacitors (the little cylinders). If they look swollen or are leaking, the board is dying. Also, use a can of compressed air to remove dust from the slots.
